How the referral model works (simple explanation)

A referral program is different from “one-time signup bounties.” In most cases, you earn when the referred users actually use the platform (trade, pay fees, create assets). That’s why your content quality matters.

1) You share your referral link

Add it inside a review page, a tutorial, or your “Tools” directory listing. Keep it visible but not spammy.

2) User signs up and starts using MintMe

You get results when users are active. Focus on the right audience: builders, creators, and traders who actually place trades.

3) You track performance & optimize

Monitor clicks, signups, and which pages convert. Your best ROI usually comes from one or two “money” pages, not everywhere.

Content angles that convert (for crypto affiliate sites)

Use one angle per page. Don’t mix 5 promises; keep it clean and believable.

Angle A: “MintMe for creators”

Make a step-by-step guide: creating a token, setting a profile, understanding fees, and how to avoid rookie mistakes. This attracts high-intent traffic.

Best traffic sources: SEO (how-to), YouTube description links, Telegram communities.

Angle B: “MintMe as a micro-cap marketplace”

Write a neutral review: pros, cons, who should avoid it, and how to manage risk. This converts better than hype.

Best traffic sources: “Best alternatives” lists, comparison pages, newsletter.
